👩🏻‍💻 Looking at Data¶

ℹ️ About the Dataset¶

  • Germany data only 🇩🇪
  • As per §15-18 Schwangerschaftskonfliktsgesetz (SchKG), abortion data is officially collected by the The Federal Statistical Office - institutions conducting abortions are obliged to provide data
  • Data is available since 1996, by calendar quarter, includes 7 characteristics, and only aggregated (further docu in the quality report)

In sum, it's a full dataset choosing §16 (1) SchKG characteristics 1. conduction of abortions, 2. legal conditions for abortion (counseling regulation or according to indication), and 3. family status, age, and number of children of pregnant women.

🔎 Legal Conditions For Abortion¶

As per §218 Strafgesetzbuch (StGB), abortions are part of the German criminal law under offences against life and incur a penalty of imprisonment or fine.

§218a excemptions are:

  1. counseling regulation: when the pregnant woman requests the abortion and has sought counseling, the abortion is conducted by a physician, and no more than 12w have passed since conception

  2. medical indication: when the health of the pregnant woman is endangered and the danger cannot be averted in any other reasonable way

  3. criminological indication: when the pregnancy is consequence of rape or sexual abuse

  4. like (1) with a 10w extension but only the pregnant woman does not incur penalty (meaning to go abroad) and if the pregnant woman was in exceptional distress at the time of the procedure
